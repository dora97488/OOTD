Editorial Vibe App Design Guideline

Warm editorial calm.

This guideline translates your design tokens and style notes into a living reference page. The system is built to feel like an interactive lifestyle magazine: paper-like surfaces, quiet serif rhythm, soft white cards, collage composition, and terracotta actions used with restraint.

Build the app as a warm editorial lifestyle magazine, not as a dashboard.
Tone and signal
Curated, tactile, feminine.

The interface should feel art-directed rather than optimized for density. White space, crop, and typography create hierarchy before controls do.

Color system

Paper first, accents last.

Base the UI on warm whites, canvas neutrals, and near-black text. Terracotta is reserved for the most intentional actions. Imagery-led accents like tomato, leaf, gold, and cream should support the mood, not dominate the layout.

Typography

Serif hierarchy with quiet utility.

Use editorial serif for titles, navigation, tabs, statistics, and emphasis. Use a restrained sans-serif for functional descriptions and compact UI detail. Metadata can lean italic and delicate.

Usage rules

Do less, but art-direct more.

The system is strongest when it feels restrained and composed. Use real white space, keep the palette disciplined, and allow a small number of expressive moments to carry the identity.

Do

  • Use warm white backgrounds and near-black editorial text.
  • Let serif typography carry page hierarchy and navigation.
  • Use terracotta only for the clearest, highest-priority action.
  • Compose content with asymmetry, crop, and generous negative space.
  • Choose illustrations and cutout imagery that feel tactile and curated.

Don't

  • Do not turn the interface into a dashboard or productivity app.
  • Do not stack cards inside cards without a clear editorial reason.
  • Do not rely on glossy effects, loud gradients, or hard contrast.
  • Do not overfill empty space; calm is part of the visual identity.
  • Do not let metadata or icons overpower the main story.
